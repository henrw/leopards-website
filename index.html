Revolutionizing online learning experiences for Africa EdTech Hub Fellows

Objective

CMU and our partners at The MasterCard Foundation's (MCF) Center for Innovative Teaching and Learning (CITL) in Africa will investigate the learning experience of EdTech Hub Fellows across Africa to identify their current learning challenges and opportunities. Based on user research and learning science theories, we will build a more supportive ecosystem around the current CMU Online certificate courses for learners to efficiently complete an online education that is contextualized and highly applicable to their personal learning objectives and entrepreneurial efforts as they pertain to the science of learning.

Our Approach

Our project spans 7 months with Lean methodology, featuring 3 MVPs to rapidly validate assumptions and iterations. Hover on different stages in the diagram for details!

Desktop Research

What do Hubs typically offer: Even though it might vary from hub to hub, most of the incubator programs are 6-months long and provides comprehensive start-up development support including funding, facilitating market access, impact measurement, improving financial sustainability, and access to certifications and educational resources.
The “Train the Trainer” model : The long-term vision for the certificate course project is to equip the Hub staff with the essential learning science knowledge so that they can advise the coming cohorts independently from CMU’s professional support. This way they can create a Community of Practice model across Africa.
